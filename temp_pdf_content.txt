摘 要 无源光网络（PON）的大规模部署对链路损耗预算提出了严苛要求，尤其是强度调
制直接检测（IM/DD）PON。在长距离场景中受制于光纤色散带来的损伤；在高发射功率下
受制于受激布里渊散射（SBS）等光纤非线性效应影响。本文提出一种基于偏振复用光双二
进制（DP-ODB）发射架构，在色散与功率两个维度拓展链路预算。ODB 信号相比PAM4 有显
著的色散优势和鲁棒性。此外，进一步将信号放到两个正交的偏振分量上，降低单个支路
的功率，从而将系统SBS 非线性容忍上限由PAM4 的7 dBm 提升至16 dBm，增幅达9 dB。
在发射功率超过10 dBm 的工况下，链路损耗预算在42.5 ps/nm 色散处可达33 dB，在170 
ps/nm 色散处仍保持29 dB。实验还验证了50 GBaud 条件下的初步可行性，表明所提方案
在迈向100G IM/DD PON 中具有显著潜力。 
关键词 无源光网络；光双二进制；偏振复用；光纤非线性

1 引  言 
随着接入网与短距网络的流量快速扩张，业界正将单波长PON 数据速率从25 Gbps 向
50 Gbps 和100 Gbps 推进。ITU-T 与IEEE 已完成50G-PON 标准化
[1-3]，该物理层保留了强度
调制直接检测（IM/DD）架构，于此类系统中首次引入了数字信号处理
[4]。与此同时，100G-
PON 的标准化进程仍在推进中，其关键技术正处于广泛研究阶段
[5-7]。相干技术虽能以大功率
预算与高色散容忍能力带来明显的性能优势
[8-10]，但其成本、功耗和复杂度，尤其是光网络
单元（ONU）侧的要求与PON 标准不符。因此在IM/DD 技术的前提下，进一步探索实现单波
长100 Gbps PON 的有效途径具有重要意义
[11]。 
高速IM/DD PON 面临的主要挑战包括带宽受限、信道损伤和功率预算受限三个方面
[12]。
由于高带宽光器件的发展总是滞后于数据速率的演进，因此问题转为如何提高频谱效率，高
阶调制格式被纳入考虑范围，但对器件的线性度有硬性要求
[13]。色散（CD）也需要重点考量。
常规下行链路采用1342 nm 波长来降低色散带来的功率损失，然而50G PON 下行链路波长
与EPON 存在冲突
[14]，在波长向更长波段偏移时，色散系数上升导致的功率预算代价需要重
新评估。为了减轻色散的影响，接收机例如Kramers–Kronig（KK）接收机
[15]和多种均衡等
多方向上的技术相继被引入。然而，KK 接收机依赖高载波功率，会牺牲信噪比（SNR）和线
性度；新兴的神经网络（NN）均衡器的研究则需要解决复杂度
[16]与数据标注问题
[17]。面对功

数也更少。

[24]，非线性容限主要取决于每路
偏振的功率，而非总发射功率。一旦突破SBS 阈值，其引发的后向散射功率会导致系统误码

以下，提供额外的功率预算。此外，如图2(a)所示，ODB 通常呈现载波抑制频谱；与PAM4
携带强残余载波、光功率集中于载波频率附近不同，无载波的ODB 降低了峰值功率集中度，
这一频谱特性使ODB 在本质上比PAM4 具有更强的非线性容忍能力。

4 结  论 
本文提出了一种双偏振双二进制系统，能够缓解直接检测PON 中的码间串扰与非线性
失真。通过将发复杂度转移至发射端，本方案实现了算法资源需求较低的简单直接检测接收
机，并确保与现有ONU 的兼容性。与PAM4 相比，DP-ODB 格式在高SNR 下表现相当，但在低
SNR场景下，尤其是高累积色散条件下对码间串扰鲁棒性显著更强。实验验证了在42.5 ps/nm
累积色散处达到−23 dBm 的接收灵敏度，在170 ps/nm 处达到−19 dBm。此外，本文测试了
对SBS 的容忍度，最后得出当发射功率超过10 dBm 时，分别实现了33 dB 和29 dB 的功率
预算，证实了该方案在PON 系统中的可行性。

Dispersion-resilient Dual-polarization Optical Duobinary with Enhanced 
Spectral Efficiency for Direct Detection PON 
Zhao Zhanglu1, Zhengxuan Li*, Siyu Luo, Yingxiong Song 
1 Key Laboratory Specialty Fiber Optics and Optical Access Networks, Joint 
International Research Laboratory of Specialty Fiber Optics and Advanced 
Communication, Shanghai University, Shanghai 200444, China 
Abstract   
Objective The continuous expansion of data-intensive services requires scaling passive 
optical network (PON) capacities from single-wavelength 25 Gbps to 50 Gbps and eventually 
100 Gbps. ITU-T and IEEE have completed the 50G-PON standardization. The physical 
layer retains the intensity-modulation direct-detection (IM/DD) architecture and introduces 
digital signal processing (DSP). Achieving such high capacities over IM/DD PON introduces 
severe constraints concerning chromatic dispersion (CD) and power budgets. Shifting 
downstream wavelengths to avoid conflicts with existing EPON systems inevitably increases 
the CD, amplifying the penalty on the power budget. Increasing the optical launch power 
provides a straightforward method to improve the power budgets, but this approach is 
fundamentally restricted by fiber nonlinearities. Specifically, stimulated Brillouin scattering 
(SBS) becomes the dominant impairment when launch power exceeds approximately 10 
dBm. Standard formats like PAM4 are particularly vulnerable to SBS because their 
unsuppressed optical carrier concentrates high power within a narrow spectral region. 
Coherent technologies offer significant performance advantages through large power budgets 
and high dispersion tolerance. However, their cost, power consumption, and complexity, 
particularly at the optical network unit (ONU), are incompatible with PON standards. To 
conclude, overcoming dispersion limits and elevating the SBS threshold requires a novel, 
backward-compatible transmission strategy. 
Methods A dual-polarization optical duobinary (DP-ODB) transmission architecture 
effectively expands transmission capacity while preserving the format's inherent tolerance to 
chromatic dispersion and fiber nonlinearities. The fundamental optical duobinary modulation 
compresses the signal spectrum and introduces a controlled intersymbol interference to 
reshape the channel memory. The precoding and 0 
−
 characteristics ensure that adjacent 
pulses representing an active state are separated by zero-energy slots. This structural property 
promotes destructive interference in the pulse tails during fiber transmission, significantly 
suppressing energy leakage and enhancing overall dispersion tolerance. To double the 
spectral efficiency without requiring a coherent receiver, the signal is mapped onto two 
orthogonal polarization states. The experimental setup utilizes an arbitrary waveform 
generator outputting 176 mV signals, which are amplified to drive a 25 GHz dual-drive 
Mach-Zehnder modulator biased at the null point. The resulting optical signal is divided into 
two branches. One branch is delayed by 20 symbols to ensure data decorrelation, while its 
power is attenuated by 3 dB to establish a strict 2:1 power ratio between the horizontal and 


=== 第13页 ===
vertical polarizations. A polarization beam combiner merges these streams before 
transmission over single-mode fiber. At the receiver end, an erbium-doped fiber amplifier and 
a single 35 GHz PIN photodiode execute direct detection. The photodiode captures the scalar 
sum of both polarizations, executing a square-law detection that directly yields a four-level 
intensity signal. This non-coherent superposition completely avoids the need for complex 90° 
optical hybrid mixers at the receiver. 
Results and Discussions The performance of the DP-ODB architecture demonstrates 
profound improvements when compared against standard PAM4 modulation across multiple 
parameters. Extensive digital signal processing optimization reveals that DP-ODB requires a 
significantly less complex equalizer structure. While PAM4 necessitates 25 feed-forward, 1 
decision-feedback, and 3 Volterra taps to function near its limit, DP-ODB achieves superior 
stability using only 25 feed-forward and 3 decision-feedback taps without any nonlinear 
Volterra terms. Evaluating the dispersion tolerance shows that the PAM4 signal reaches the 
operational limit at 127.5 ps/nm of accumulated dispersion. The DP-ODB format extends this 
limit to 170 ps/nm that substantially reduces the need for dispersion compensation in short-
reach links. Assessing receiver sensitivity yields equally compelling data. At the optimal 
dispersion point of 42.5 ps/nm, the DP-ODB system achieves a sensitivity of -23 dBm. This 
represents a 3 dB improvement over the back-to-back baseline of PAM4. Even at the elevated 
dispersion of 170 ps/nm, DP-ODB maintains a highly capable sensitivity of -19 dBm, 
proving its robust tolerance to inter-symbol interference. Transitioning the focus to the optical 
power dimension, splitting the signal across two polarizations fundamentally alters the 
nonlinear dynamics within the fiber. Each polarization branch operates below the strong SBS 
gain region. Consequently, the system's SBS tolerance ceiling increases dramatically from 7 
dBm for PAM4 to 16 dBm for DP-ODB. This distinct 9 dB enhancement stems from two 
complementary mechanisms. While the carrier-suppressed nature of ODB coding delivers the 
primary advantage by contributing roughly 7 dB, the physical division of optical power 
accounts for the remaining 2 dB. Operating at launch powers exceeding 10 dBm allows the 
system to realize immense link loss budgets. The optimal working point secures a 33 dB 
budget, and the system retains a robust 29 dB budget even under 170 ps/nm of accumulated 
dispersion. 
Conclusions The proposed dual-polarization optical duobinary configuration successfully 
mitigates the dual threats of chromatic dispersion and stimulated Brillouin scattering inherent 
in high-speed access networks. Consolidating the architectural complexity at the optical line 
terminal ensures that the remote optical network units remain highly simplified and 
economically viable for widespread deployment. The system establishes remarkable link loss 
budgets of up to 33 dB, ensuring complete compatibility with long-reach optical distribution 
networks demanding high split ratios. Elevating the transmission power without triggering 
catastrophic nonlinear penalties proves that direct-detection architectures can be extended 
well beyond their traditional physical boundaries. Preliminary validation at 50 GBaud further 
confirms the scalability of this architecture. This framework delivers a highly practical, 
standard-compatible pathway for deploying next-generation 100G single-wavelength passive 
optical networks.
