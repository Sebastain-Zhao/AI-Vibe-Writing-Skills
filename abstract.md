Industrial automation and the Internet of Things (IoT) are driving unprecedented demand for optical access networks capable of supporting massive connectivity with deterministic low latency. Conventional passive optical networks (PONs) face significant scalability and cost limitations in ultra-dense deployment scenarios. To address these challenges, we propose and experimentally demonstrate a polarization–carrier dual-reuse coherent orthogonal frequency division multiplexing PON (CO-OFDM-PON) architecture enabled by optical frequency comb (OFC) sources. The proposed design maps data and carrier signals onto orthogonal polarization states, enabling carrier reuse for both downstream coherent detection and upstream transmission through injection-locked laser (ILL)-based carrier regeneration at the optical network unit (ONU). Key enabling subsystems—including the OFC, ILL, and pre-amplified erbium-doped fiber amplifier (EDFA)—are comprehensively characterized to ensure stable multiwavelength generation, cost-effective carrier regeneration, and enhanced receiver sensitivity under high split ratios. Through comprehensive simulation and experimental analysis of fiber nonlinearities, we identify an optimal per-channel launch power of 4 dBm that effectively balances sensitivity and link budget requirements. Experimental validation over 5 km of standard single-mode fiber (SSMF) demonstrates 16×100Gb/s/λ downstream 16-QAM OFDM transmission with a link budget exceeding 34 dB and bit error rates (BER) below the forward error correction (FEC) threshold of 1 × 10⁻². These results validate the feasibility and scalability of the proposed CO-OFDM-PON architecture for addressing massive connectivity and deterministic low-latency requirements in industrial automation and IoT scenarios, establishing it as a cost-effective and energy-efficient solution for next-generation ultra-dense industrial optical access networks.
